TO THE BEACON
GAINS for Teens · A Single-Session Intervention

The Long Light

A dream that rehearses the bravest thing you’ll do tomorrow.
Scroll to enter the dream
The frame

Tonight, a teen can’t sleep.

In the morning they meet a new therapist for the very first time — and they’re scared. To quiet their mind, they open a book and drift into its world. Everything that follows is a dream. And the dream is the intervention.

You never see their face. You see through their eyes — until the world hands you a new one.

Zone I

The Hollow

You wake in the dark with a single light in your hand. Far above — almost too far to believe — a warm beacon. That’s where you’re going.

Start. The phone you fell asleep holding is now the light you carry. The whole journey points upward, toward the Beacon.
Zone II

The Lantern Path

A carved stone glows as you near it — a message left by someone who once stood exactly here, and made it through.

Psychoeducation, delivered as a short message from someone who’s been in your shoes — discovered, not lectured.
Zone III

The Mistfields

Higher now, above the first clouds. The path is harder — but you’re not the first to walk it. Lanterns and stones light the way.

An activity to make the lesson yours — then a brief, playful crossing to the next light.
Zone IV

The Bright Reaches

You break above the clouds. The light you’ve gathered along the way is paying off — and the world is warm now.

Gear up. Everything you’ve collected makes the last climb easier. Progress you can see.
Zone V

The Threshold

The Beacon. You climb the last steps, and the door opens into pure light.

The lighthouse was the therapist’s door all along.
And then — morning

You wake. You’re ready to go.

“Come on — time to get ready for our appointment.” The dream rehearsed the real thing.

A single-session intervention for a teen about to start trauma therapy — psychoeducation and practice, carried by a game you ascend rather than a lesson you sit through. Built on the SSI Platform.
